Spider-Man: Homecoming Movie Explanation

  • By Editorial Staff
  • October 29, 2024 - 2 min

Spider-Man: Homecoming is Spider-Man's first solo film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. We previously saw Tom Holland's Spider-Man in Captain America: Civil War. This time, he appears with his own story, where the conflict between the life and responsibility of a teenage hero emerges.

The story begins in New York, some time after the events of the Avengers movie. A construction contractor named Adrian Toomes collects alien technology and starts making illegal weapons. He gradually becomes the terrifying villain Vulture. On the other hand, Peter Parker is still a schoolboy, but he tries to suppress crime in the city by wearing the Spider-suit given to him by Tony Stark.

Peter wants to fully join the Avengers, but Tony Stark repeatedly warns him that he is still young, he needs to learn to take responsibility. Peter does not want to accept it and starts fighting against Vulture in his own way. Meanwhile, his school life also becomes complicated, because he has to manage his studies, friends and hero life at the same time.

At one point, Peter realizes that Vulture is actually his friend's father - Liz's father. This information shakes him mentally. But instead of avoiding his responsibility, he decides to stop the villain. In the end, there is a fierce fight on a plane, where Peter stops Vulture with courage and intelligence. However, he does not kill him, but saves him and hands him over to the police.

At the end of the movie, Tony Stark offers him the opportunity to join the Avengers team, but Peter refuses. He decides to work for people in his hometown of Brooklyn. In this way, he proves that being a hero does not only mean winning big battles, but also fulfilling small responsibilities correctly.

Spider-Man: Homecoming was a light-hearted, fun, but also inspiring movie. It beautifully portrays the struggles, friendships, and responsibilities of teenagers. Tom Holland proves through this film that he is truly the modern-day Spider-Man.
